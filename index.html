interesting facts about Nikola Tesla

  • Early Innovations: Tesla was born in 1856 in Serbia. He showed an early interest in electrical and mechanical engineering, even creating his first invention, a small water turbine, at the age of 5.
  • Tesla and His Pigeon Friends: Tesla had a close relationship with pigeons and often fed them in the park. He claimed to have a special bond with a specific white pigeon and believed that the bird had a connection with him.
  • Tesla's Photographic Memory:Tesla had a remarkable photographic memory. He could visualize and work out complex designs and calculations in his head without the need for detailed notes or diagrams. .
  • War of the Currents: Tesla's AC system competed with Thomas Edison's direct current (DC) system in what became known as the "War of the Currents." Tesla's AC ultimately proved to be more efficient and safer for long-distance power transmission.
  • Obsession with the Number 3: Tesla had a peculiar obsession with the number 3 and often engaged in rituals involving numbers divisible by it. He would often walk around a building three times before entering and When staying in hotels, he had specific preferences for room numbers that were divisible by 3.
  • Teslaphobia - Fear of Pearls: Tesla had an unusual fear of pearls. He believed that wearing or even seeing pearls would bring him bad luck.

    Prominent Achievements:

  • AC Power Transmission System: Tesla's alternating current system revolutionized electrical power distribution, making it possible to generate electricity centrally and transmit it efficiently over long distances.
  • Tesla Coil and Wireless Energy Transfer: The Tesla coil allowed for the generation of high-voltage, low-current electricity and paved the way for wireless energy transmission technologies.
  • Remote Control and Robotics: Tesla demonstrated the first remote-controlled model boat and envisioned the use of radio waves for remote control and automation, which has since become a critical technology.
